That first line of code feeling

31 May 2019

I have enrolled in Treehouses Techdegree in front-end development.
8 minutes and 42 seconds completed of “HTML and CSS Basics.” In this time I have managed to transform text from yellow to white, expand an image when you hover over it and finally understand what HTML and CSS stand for.
Hyper-text…something something right?
However it’s not the actual task that is the accomplishment here. It is the feeling I got when I clicked command+s on my CSS stylesheet followed by command+r on my web browser and somehow like magic I, yes I, turned the text from yellow to white. The rush through my entire body was unreal. I sat up in my chair and moved closer to my laptop. Frantically clicked away at the “next lesson” button because that was a rush and I need another hit. Give me the next line of code to tweak, teach me how to animate my images, when can I make things flash, spin and jump out of my screen!? Next lesson is surely virtual reality? I paused after I completed what is seen as a very simple task. I took in the feeling, the moment. Because I know as I advance, simple CSS or HTML tweaks will no longer give me that same satisfaction. However I think this is an important lesson in itself.
Take on every moment, task and interaction in life like you are writing your first line of code.
